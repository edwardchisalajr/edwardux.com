PORTFOLIO

Coalition of Manitoba Cultural Communities for Families (CMCCF) |

Role

UX/UI Designer and Researcher

Context

CMCCF is a nonprofit organisation supporting diverse cultural and minority communities across Manitoba, Canada. Their website shares community events and organisational updates. CMCCF fosters inclusion and empowerment by connecting members to vital services and opportunities.

Problem

The current website has poor navigation, limited mobile responsiveness, and accessibility issues, hindering diverse users from finding information and engaging effectively.

Approach

Approached the redesign through usability analysis, restructuring content, and simplifying user flows to improve accessibility, clarity and mobile responsiveness.

Outcome

The redesigned platform improved overall usability and accessibility, resulting in a more engaging experience for users across 20+ cultural communities. While providing an easier way to navigate on both desktop and mobile devices.

Cyberviolence Awareness Mobile Application | UNESCO Chair (Namibia) in Digital Technologies |

Role

Software Developer, Research Support

Context

The UNESCO Chair in Digital Technologies focuses on research and interventions addressing digital safety, cyberviolence and internet governance. The project aimed to develop a mobile application to raise awareness of cyberviolence and support digital safety education.

Problem

Cyberviolence resources are fragmented and inaccessible, thus creating a need for a user-friendly digital tool to deliver sensitive information clearly and responsibly.

Approach

Developed the app’s design and development by translating user research into intuitive interfaces, structuring content and supporting implementation with a focus on simplicity and accessibility.

Outcome

The application was successfully deployed, it supported workshops, NGO collaborations, and national-level digital safety initiatives, contributing to broader conversations on online safety and cyberviolence prevention.

Digital Platforms and AI-Supported Systems | African Development Bank (AfDB) |

Role

Software and Systems Development

Context

The AfDB is a multilateral institution supporting economic development and social progress across Africa. Their mandate focuses on development priorities: powering the continent, feeding its people, industrializing economies, integrating regions, and enhancing quality of life.

Problem

Existing processes required more efficient digital tools to improve usability, data handling, and system reliability, with regards to manual and inefficient AI CV screening.

Approach

Develop a AI web based application through machine learning, UI implementation and system testing to ensure usability, security, and performance.

Outcome

The systems supported more efficient digital workflows and contributed to inclusive employment and data-driven decision-making initiatives within the institution. While aligning with the Bank’s broader development and SDG-focused objectives.
